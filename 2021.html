Chart 3: Suspect → Victim Race Pairings (Specific)
Each bar = one specific pairing (e.g. "Black suspect → Black victim"). Green = intraracial, Red = interracial. Only pairings with 1+ cases shown.
Finding: Black-on-Black violence accounted for 17 of 25 solved pairings (68%). Hispanic suspects killing Latina victims accounted for 2 cases. White-on-White pairings accounted for 3 cases. Cross-racial pairings were less common: Black suspect→Latina victim (2 cases), Hispanic suspect→Black victim (1), White suspect→Native American victim (1).

Chart 5: Bias Motive Coding
All 47 victims coded by bias motive evidence
Methodology: Confirmed = hate crime charges or explicit LE statement. Suspected = evidence of animus, no charge (e.g., dating app lure, trans panic indicators, DA statement of probable bias). No Evidence = documented alternative motive (IPV, robbery, familicide, mental illness). Unknown = insufficient info or unsolved with no motive data.

Methodology: Victim list from HRC's 2021 Fatal Violence Report (47 victims at publication). All case details independently verified via local news and court records. HRC assessments not used for bias motive, suspect info, or case status. Unsolved cases searched for updates through February 2026 (e.g., Kiér Laprí Kartier's suspect arrested October 2025; EJ Boykin's suspect arrested January 2023). Police-involved killing (Krys Brandon Ruiz) excluded from solve rates and suspect demographics. Bright/Cannady familicide coded "No Evidence" per mother's mental health crisis — not bias-motivated. Bias coding follows strict evidentiary standard: Confirmed requires hate crime charges; Suspected requires documented animus evidence; No Evidence requires documented alternative motive; Unknown for insufficient information. Suspect race marked with asterisk (*) indicates strong circumstantial evidence (mugshot photos, witness statements) but not yet confirmed via official booking records — author should verify via FDLE or PA DOC databases.
