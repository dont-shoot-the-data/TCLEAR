Chart 3: Suspect → Victim Race Pairings (Specific)
Each bar = one specific pairing (e.g. "Black suspect → Black victim"). Green = intraracial, Red = interracial. Only pairings with 1+ cases shown. 14 solved cases with known races for both suspect and victim.
Finding: Intraracial violence dominates: 10 of 14 pairings (71.4%) are same-race. Black-on-Black violence accounts for 7 cases (50% of all pairings). Interracial pairings include Black susp.→Latina vic. (2), Black susp.→White vic. (1), Hispanic susp.→Black vic. (1).
Chart 4: Solved vs. Unsolved by Victim Race
Racial disparity in case resolution (all 30 homicides)
Key disparity: Black victims have a 55% solve rate (11 of 20), while White victims have a 100% solve rate (5 of 5) and Latina victims have a 75% solve rate (3 of 4). The disparity in case resolution mirrors national trends.
Chart 5: Bias Motive Coding
All 30 victims coded by bias motive evidence
Methodology: Confirmed = hate crime charges or explicit LE statement. Suspected = evidence of animus, no charge. No Evidence = documented alternative motive. Unknown = insufficient info. No cases received hate crime charges in 2024. Three cases have suspected bias: Kitty Monroe (family suspects anti-trans targeting), Tayy Dior Thomas (boyfriend hiding trans relationship), and Monique Brooks (passenger told investigators victim was targeted for being transgender).

Methodology: Victim list from HRC's 2024 Fatal Violence Report (30 calendar year 2024 victims). All details independently verified via local news and court records. HRC assessments not used for case status, bias motive, or suspect info. Unsolved cases searched for arrest updates through February 2026. Police-involved killings excluded from solve rates and suspect demographics. Suspect race verified from mugshots or explicit police/news descriptions where available. Of 15 racially identified suspects, 4 confirmed by police records/booking photos, 8 consistent with available evidence, 3 unverifiable.
